Packing for Business Travel

Novice travelers should focus on packing efficiently, having access to basic resources through smartphone apps, and knowing how to check-in before a flight. Mobile apps are excellent tools for travelers of all experience levels, so be sure to download the best and most useful traveling apps before your next trip to be ready to apply them to any given situation. Frequent business traveler Chuck Gose additionally offers several helpful tips on packing efficiently, including only bringing one pair of shoes (the ones on your feet) and choosing versatile clothing items.

Common Misconceptions about Business Travel

Much of your intimidation about business travel may be nothing but an illusion, since there are several common misconceptions that surround the concept of business travel. As Sandra Eblevi of HCC Medical Insurance Services points out, business travelers don't necessarily have to fly business class, nor do they have to be greeted at the airport by a private limo driver.

These misconceptions arenâ€™t reflective of what traveling is actually like for most professionals. This association likely stems from the fact that business travel used to be reserved for especially successful companies; but today, companies of all sizes can travel virtually anywhere in the world for business. Since these companies often don't come from extravagant backgrounds, being economically-efficient with business travel is becoming more and more common. Read more about business travel myths.

Travel Tips for the Savvy Professional

In addition to the travel tips listed above, there are several additional tips that professionals should adhere to in order to foster a successful trip. These tips include the following:

  • Purchase medical travel insurance to have proper coverage in the event of a health emergency
  • Research common phrases, cultural customs, and potential offenses to enhance communication and prevent creating a negative image during your business meeting
  • Use LinkedIn to connect with other professionals in your destination and post questions to the answer board to receive helpful advice from professionals who have been there before you

Although traveling for business is technically considered "work," it can still be pleasurable if you come prepared. In addition to experiencing a new land and culture, you can increase your outreach to professionals in your industry on a worldwide scale. Doing so not only expands your network, but also helps to enhance your resume and provides opportunities to learn from businesses that operate in entirely new environments.
